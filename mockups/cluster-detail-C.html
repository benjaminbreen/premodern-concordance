Wikipedia
Brazil, officially the Federative Republic of Brazil, is the largest country in South America and in Latin America. At 8.5 million square kilometers, Brazil is the world's fifth-largest country by area. With around 214 million people, Brazil is the world's sixth-most populous country.

In the early modern period, Brazil was a Portuguese colony known for its production of sugar, brazilwood dye, and later gold. It attracted significant attention from European naturalists and physicians interested in its unique flora, fauna, and indigenous medical knowledge.
